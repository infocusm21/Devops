To **highlight an entire line** in the terminal, you can use different methods depending on your terminal type:

---

### **1. Using Keyboard Shortcuts**
#### **In Most Linux Terminals (GNOME Terminal, Konsole, etc.):**  
- Move the cursor to the beginning of the line.
- Press **`Shift + Home`** → Highlights from the cursor to the start.
- Press **`Shift + End`** → Highlights from the cursor to the end.
- Press **`Ctrl + A`** → Moves the cursor to the start of the line.
- Press **`Ctrl + E`** → Moves the cursor to the end.

#### **For zsh / bash with Readline:**
- **Alt + Shift + →** → Select forward word-by-word.
- **Alt + Shift + ←** → Select backward word-by-word.

---

### **2. Using Mouse**
- Click at the beginning of the line, then drag to the end.
- **Triple-click** anywhere on the line to highlight the entire line.

---

### **3. In `vim` or `nano` (Text Editors)**
#### **For `vim`:**
- Move to the desired line.
- Press `V` (uppercase) → Highlights the entire line.
- Move up/down to extend the selection.
  
#### **For `nano`:**
- Move to the line.
- Press `Ctrl + 6` → Start selection.
- Use arrow keys to highlight.

---

### **4. In `tmux` (Terminal Multiplexer)**
- Press **`Ctrl + B`, then `[`** to enter copy mode.
- Move to the start of the line.
- Press **`Space`** to start selection.
- Move to the end and press **`Enter`**.
